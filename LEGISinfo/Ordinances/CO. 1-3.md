# CO. 1-3 Harrison County Parks and Recreation Ordinance

*Introduced January 31, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

January 31, 2026 Mr. Vinnyvxx introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To establish the Harrison County Department of Parks and Recreation; to provide for its organization, powers, and duties; to authorize the appointment of a Director and Park Rangers; to provide for the administration, conservation, and protection of County parks, lands, waters, and recreational facilities; to authorize permitting and limited enforcement within County lands.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as the "Harrison County Parks and Recreation Ordinance" or the "HCPR Ordinance".

---

## SECTION 2. HARRISON COUNTY DEPARTMENT OF PARKS AND RECREATION; DIRECTOR

(a) There is an executive department of Harrison County, the Harrison County Department of Parks and Recreation ("Department" or "HCPR"), responsible for the administration, operation, maintenance, and stewardship of County parks, trails, recreational facilities, waterways, nature reserves, natural areas, and other public lands or facilities placed under its care by Resolution of the Council (collectively, "County lands and facilities administered by HCPR").

> (i) HCPR is further designated as the County department with countywide responsibility for the regulation, administration, and stewardship of fishing, hunting, wildlife, conservation, and other natural resource-related activities, regardless of land ownership, as authorized by County law.

(b) There is a Director of Parks and Recreation ("Director") who shall be appointed by the County Executive, confirmed by the Council, and shall exercise such authority as may be necessary to carry out the Director's duties under this Ordinance and other County law.

(c) The Department may establish and remove such divisions, programs, and services as are necessary to carry out its responsibilities, including conservation, outdoor recreation, public education, facility operations, and emergency readiness.

---

## SECTION 3. POWERS; PARK ADMINISTRATION; CONSERVATION

(a) The Department shall manage County lands and facilities administered by HCPR by establishing and administering rules and permit systems governing access, hours, camping, reservations, facility use, recreational activities, environmental protection, visitor conduct, and the lawful use, taking, and conservation of fish, wildlife, and other natural resources, where permitted by law.

(b) The Department shall provide and coordinate public safety and protective services within County lands and facilities administered by HCPR, including patrol, first aid, water safety and rescue, search and rescue, emergency response, and the enforcement of applicable County laws, rules, and regulations.

---

## SECTION 4. PARK RANGERS; AUTHORITY AND DUTIES

(a) The Director may employ, appoint, and remove Park Rangers and other personnel as necessary to carry out the functions of the Department, in accordance with applicable law, the Charter, and County personnel policies.

(b) Park Rangers are not peace officers by virtue of this Ordinance and shall not make arrests; however, when assigned or acting in an enforcement, investigative, protective, or public safety capacity, and in addition to any other duties assigned under this Ordinance, Park Rangers are authorized, within the scope of their official duties, to exercise the following powers and responsibilities:

> (i) Provide public safety and protective services within County lands and facilities administered by HCPR, including patrol, monitoring of conditions, and actions reasonably necessary to preserve order, protect persons and property, and ensure safe and lawful use of such lands and facilities;
>
> (ii) Investigate violations of, and enforce, the Park Code and any other applicable County laws, rules, or regulations relating to parks, recreation, conservation, wildlife, hunting, fishing, off-road or recreational vehicle use, and the use and protection of natural resources, including by issuing citations, notices of violation, and orders to comply, and by directing persons to cease prohibited conduct or to leave County lands and facilities administered by HCPR when necessary to enforce such laws or protect public safety;
>
> (iii) Investigate and enforce County laws and regulations relating to hunting, fishing, wildlife, conservation, and the protection of natural resources throughout Harrison County, provided that such authority shall not include the power to make arrests;
>
> (iv) Temporarily detain a person when the Ranger has probable cause to believe that the person has committed an offense in the Ranger's presence, or presents an immediate and articulable threat to life, serious bodily harm, or imminent and substantial harm to public safety, provided that any such detention is limited to the period reasonably necessary to stabilize the situation and transfer custody to the Sheriff or other lawful authority;
>
> (v) Respond to emergencies or public safety incidents outside County lands and facilities administered by HCPR when requested or directed by the Sheriff's Office or another lawful public safety agency, or when the Ranger directly observes conduct presenting an immediate and articulable threat to life or serious bodily harm, and take reasonable action consistent with subparagraph (iv) to protect life and safety until relieved by sworn law enforcement or proper emergency personnel;
>
> (vi) Carry and use such equipment as is authorized by the Department and reasonably necessary for the performance of assigned duties, operate Department-authorized vehicles, including emergency vehicles, and use reasonable and proportionate force when necessary to carry out lawful duties, prevent resistance, protect themselves or others, or effectuate a lawful detention under this Ordinance, consistent with Department policy and applicable law;
>
> (vii) Coordinate with the Sheriff's Office and comply with lawful requests of the District Attorney made in furtherance of prosecution, consistent with the Charter and County law.

---

## SECTION 6. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
