# CO. 1-14 Evasion Ordinance

*Introduced February 14, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

February 14, 2026 Mr. king_v123 introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To Criminalize Evading Lawful Authority.

*Be it resolved by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as the "Evasion Ordinance".

---

## SECTION 2. ENFORCEMENT

(a) Evasion shall be added to Title 1 Chapter 1 of H.C.C. as "Whoever intentionally flees or attempts to flee from a uniformed peace officer, provided that the peace officer informs them to stop, shall be guilty of Evasion."

- (i) Evasion shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 9 minutes in the County Jail or a $1500 fine.

(b) Felony Evasion shall be added to Title 1 Chapter 1 of H.C.C. as "Whoever intentionally flees or attempts to flee from a uniformed peace officer while also endangering the lives of others, provided that the peace officer informs them to stop, shall be guilty of Felony Evasion."

- (i) Felony Evasion shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 15 minutes in the County Jail.

(c) Resisting Arrest shall be added to Title 1 Chapter 1 of H.C.C. as "Whoever intentionally prevents an arrest, regardless of its legality, by creating a substantial risk of bodily injury to the peace officer or anyone else, shall be guilty of Resisting Arrest"

- (i) Resisting Arrest shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 7 minutes in the County Jail or a $750 fine.

(d) Failure to comply shall be added to Title 1 Chapter 1 of H.C.C. as "Whoever intentionally fails to comply with peace officers' lawful orders, shall be guilty of Failure to Comply."

- (i) Failure to Comply shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 3 minutes in the County Jail or a $300 fine.

(e) For the purposes of Section 2 (a) and (b):

- (i) "Fleeing" shall be defined as running, driving away or escaping any form of detainment by peace officers;

- (ii) A peace officer may inform a person to stop by using their:

  - (1) Voice,

  - (2) Emergency lighting,

  - (3) Emergency sirens,

  - (4) Any combination of the above.

- (iii) A uniformed peace officer shall be defined as any person bearing the badge of a Law Enforcement Agency.

(f) For the purposes of Section 2 (c) the element of preventing an arrest shall apply to preventing an arrest of a handcuffed person made aware of their arrest.

(g) For the purposes of Section 2 (d) "lawful orders" shall only cover orders that serve to:

- (i) Prevent a crime from happening,

- (ii) Detain a person,

- (iii) Process an arrest,

- (iv) Process a lawful search of a person, vehicle or a residence,

- (v) Move a detainee,

- (vi) Execute a warrant.

---
