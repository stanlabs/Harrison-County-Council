# CO. 1-5 Roadway Traffic Act

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

A0IM introduced the following Bill to the Council

## A BILL

To establish the fundamental groundwork of the roadway traffic laws within the county of Harrison, including promulgating the way of driving within the county of Harrison

*Be it enacted, pursuant to Section 24 of the Harrison County Charter, by the Council here assembled-*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

This Act shall be cited as the "Roadway Traffic Act"

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) Motor Vehicle — Any self-propelled vehicle, of which the purpose is to travel on roads.

(b) Emergency Vehicle — A motor vehicle possessing emergency lights

(c) Law Enforcement Officer — An on-duty law enforcement officer

(d) Emergency Service Employee — An on-duty employee of the Harrison County Fire & Rescue, or Harrison County Parks & Recreation

(e) Military Convoy — A group of two (2) or more motor vehicles operated by, or under the authority of The Harrison county militia, travelling in a coordinated manner from one location to another, for an official military purpose

(f) Traffic Guide — A law enforcement officer, emergency service employee, or on-duty transit authority employee, who is actively directing traffic

(g) Emergency Lights — Lights capable of emitting flashing, rotating, oscillating, or strobing illumination in red or blue, whether permanently installed or temporarily mounted, designed or used to signal an emergency response, traffic priority, or law enforcement or emergency services, and activated automatically or by a dedicated emergency control system.

This term does not include standard hazard warning lights, turn signals, manually operated blinkers, decorative lighting, underglow, or lighting not intended to convey emergency or official authority.

(h) Amber Lights — Lights capable of emitting flashing, rotating, oscillating, or strobing amber (yellow) illumination, whether permanently installed or temporarily mounted, used to warn other roadway users of a stationary, slow-moving, or hazardous vehicle, roadway condition, or work activity, and activated automatically or by a dedicated warning-light control system.

This term does not include standard hazard warning lights, turn signals, parking lights, decorative or accent lighting, underglow, or manually operated blinkers not intended to convey an official warning.

---

## SECTION 3. ROAD & SIGNAGE

(a) Road — A paved path, for Motor Vehicles to travel on, including but not limited to (public) roads, motorways, and other paths which their primary purpose is to allow motor vehicles to traverse on them

(b) Interstate Highway

- (i) An interstate roadway, defined by S05 under section 3d of this act

- (ii) The speed limit of all interstate highways shall have a maximum speed limit of eighty-five (85) miles per hour

- (iii) Any motor vehicle, incapable of reaching a minimum speed of sixty (60) miles per hour, shall not be permitted on the Interstate Highway

(c) Pathway — An unpaved path, for motor vehicles to travel on

(d) Signage

- **S01 - Speed Limit** — A white, rectangle sign, displaying "SPEED LIMIT" in text, also pertaining a numeric value under said text, which is used to define the current speed limit on the road it is placed respectively.

- **S02a - Stop Sign** — A red, octagonal sign, displaying "STOP" in text, indicating that a motor vehicle must come to a full stop, and yield to all traffic.

- **S02b - Stop Sign 'All Way'** — A red, octagonal sign, displaying "STOP" in text, indicating that a motor vehicle must come to a full stop, and yield to all traffic, in addition to having a square sign displaying "ALL WAY" in text.

- **S03 - Yield Sign** — A white, rectangular sign, pointing downwards, with red edges, displaying "YIELD" in text

- **S04 - Forbidden Turn Sign** — A white, square sign, displaying an arrow, with around it a red circle with a cross, indicating a turn is prohibited.

- **S05 - Interstate Highway Sign** — A blue, shield sign, displaying the text "INTERSTATE"

- **S06 - Bus Stop Sign** — A blue, square sign, displaying a bus pictogram in white, and "BUS STOP" in white text

- **Miscellaneous Signage Clause** — Instructions on a white on black sign, in text or clearly discernable pictogram, must be obeyed

(e) Traffic Device

- **D01 - Traffic Light** — A traffic device, typically placed at intersections, of whose primary function is to direct traffic. Said device is capable of displaying a Red, Yellow, or Green signal.

---

## SECTION 4. VEHICLE REQUIREMENTS

All motor vehicles actively making use of the public roads must adhere to the regulations listed within this subchapter, otherwise they will be in violation of 'driving an unroadworthy vehicle'

A vehicle must:

- (i) Have a visible license plate, on both the front and backside

- (ii) Have correctly functioning head, and rear lights.

- (iii) Have correctly functioning brakelights

- (iv) Have correctly functioning blinkers

- (v) Have a correctly functioning rear light

- (vi) Have a correctly functioning horn

- (vii) Must not have the ability to display emergency lights

Except if that vehicle:

- (i) Is a government-owned and operated vehicle

- (ii) The vehicle is one of the following models: "Aristocrat", "First Chassis", "Z plane"

- (iii) The vehicle has a permit from the Transit Authority to be exempted from the promulgated requirements listed in this section, said permit must be readily available to be shown to a law enforcement officer upon request

---

## SECTION 5. LAW ENFORCEMENT

Law enforcement operating a motor vehicle, may at their own discretion:

- (i) Exceed the posted speed limit by twenty-five (25) miles per hour, when outside of municipal limits

- (ii) Exceed the posted speed limit by fifteen (15) miles per hour, when within municipal limits

---

## SECTION 6. MILITIA

On-duty members of the Harrison county militia operating a motor vehicle, may whilst in a military convoy:

- (i) Exceed the posted speed limit by twenty (20) miles per hour

On-duty members of the Harrison county militia, may, during an active state of emergency, be exempt from section 8 of this bill, and the Harrison Vehicle Code, granted that they operate their motor vehicles with due care

---

## SECTION 7. EMERGENCY VEHICLES

An emergency vehicle shall always have the right of way over any traffic, when they are utilizing their emergency lights, including but not limited to motor vehicles and pedestrians. Additionally, an emergency vehicle utilizing their emergency lights shall have no other traffic codes applied to them.

---

## SECTION 8. VEHICULAR OFFENSES

The following offenses shall be added to the Harrison Vehicle Code:

---

**FAILURE TO STOP AT A STOP SIGN**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, and fails to bring the vehicle to a complete stop before a stop sign, or fails to yield to all traffic at a stop sign, defined S02 under §3d, shall be guilty of failure to stop at a stop sign

- (i) Failure to stop at a stop sign is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Failure to stop at a stop sign shall warrant a citation of $275

---

**FAILURE TO YIELD**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, and fails to yield to oncoming traffic before a yield sign, defined 'S03' shall be considered guilty of failure to yield.

- (i) Failure to stop at a yield sign is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Failure to stop at a yield sign shall warrant a citation of $275

---

**SPEEDING WITHIN A MUNICIPALITY**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, whilst exceeding the posted speed limit at least five (5) miles per hour, within municipal limits shall be guilty of Speeding within a municipality.

- (i) Speeding within a municipality is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Speeding within a municipality shall warrant a citation of $355

---

**SPEEDING**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, whilst exceeding the posted speed limit at least five (5) miles per hour, outside of municipal limits, shall be guilty of Speeding

- (i) Speeding is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Speeding shall warrant a citation of $295

---

**CRIMINAL SPEEDING**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, whilst exceeding the posted speed limit at least thirty (30) miles per hour, shall be guilty of criminal speeding

- (i) Criminal speeding is a misdemeanor-type offense

- (ii) Criminal speeding shall warrant a citation of $545 or a county jail sentence of four (4) minutes

---

**RECKLESS DRIVING**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, without due regard for the safety of others shall be considered guilty of reckless driving. Furthermore, any operator of a motor vehicle who commits three (3) or more Traffic Code violations in the span of less than three (3) minutes shall also be considered guilty of reckless driving.

- (i) Reckless driving is a misdemeanor-type offense

- (ii) Reckless driving shall warrant a citation of $685, or a county jail sentence of eight (8) minutes.

---

**FELONY RECKLESS DRIVING**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, without due regard for the safety of other drivers or pedestrians, and while posing a significant threat to the lives of others shall be considered guilty of felony reckless driving

- (i) Felony reckless driving is a felony-type offense

- (ii) Felony reckless driving shall warrant a county jail sentence of twelve (12) minutes

---

**FAILURE TO YIELD TO EMERGENCY VEHICLES**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, and does not yield to emergency vehicles that are actively displaying their emergency lights, shall be guilty of failure to yield to Emergency vehicles.

- (i) Failure to yield to emergency vehicles is a misdemeanor-type offense

- (ii) Failure to yield to emergency vehicles shall warrant a citation of $765, or a county Jail sentence of six (6) minutes

---

**DRIVING IN THE WRONG LANE**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle in the opposite lane of the road, where there is a double yellow line, or whoever operates a motor vehicle driving the wrong way on an Interstate highway, shall be guilty of driving in the wrong lane

- (i) Driving in the wrong lane shall be an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Driving in the opposite lane shall warrant a citation of $260

- (iii) Whoever operates a motor vehicle in the opposite lane, on an interstate highway, shall additionally be guilty of Felony Reckless Driving

- (iv) Whoever operates a motor vehicle in the opposite lane, under the instructions of a traffic guide, shall not be liable for driving in the wrong lane

---

**WRONGFUL OPERATION OF AN EMERGENCY VEHICLE**

Any person, who is not a law enforcement officer, or emergency service employee, who operates an emergency vehicle, shall be guilty of wrongful operation of an Emergency vehicle

- (i) Wrongful operation of an emergency vehicle is a misdemeanor-type offense

- (ii) Wrongful operation of an emergency vehicle shall warrant a citation of $625, or a eight (8) minute sentence in the county jail

---

**WRONGFUL OPERATION OF AMBER LIGHTS**

Whoever operates or activates amber lights on a motor vehicle in any of the following ways:

- (i) While not actively engaged in construction, roadway maintenance, towing or roadside assistance

- (ii) While conducting routine driving, patrol, or presence operations

- (iii) While the motor vehicle is in motion and not creating, or warning to a roadway Hazard

- (iv) In a manner to imitate an emergency vehicle

Except if:

- (i) The motor vehicle is owned and operated by the government

- (ii) The motor vehicle is owned by and operated by the Harrison county waste management

- (iii) The motor vehicle is located on private property

- (iv) The motor vehicle is actively engaged in construction, roadway maintenance, towing or roadside assistance of such activity only

Shall be guilty of wrongful operation of amber lights

- (i) Wrongful operation of amber lights is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Wrongful operation of amber lights shall warrant a citation of $295

---

**FAILURE TO INDICATE**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, and fails to indicate their direction of travel at an Intersection, or who changes lanes without utilizing their blinker, shall be guilty of failure to indicate

- (i) Failure to indicate is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Failure to indicate shall warrant a citation of $150

- (iii) Except if their vehicle does not possess the ability to indicate, by lack of blinkers, and such vehicle is authorized on public roadways as described by section 4 of this act, in which case the driver shall not be guilty of failure to indicate

---

**DRIVING AN UNROADWORTHY VEHICLE**

Any person, who operates a vehicle, which does not meet the requirements listed under section 4 of this act, shall be guilty of driving an unroadworthy vehicle

- (i) Driving an unroadworthy vehicle is a misdemeanor-type offense

- (ii) Driving an unroadworthy vehicle shall warrant a citation of $725

---

**FORBIDDEN TURN**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, and makes a turn which is in violation of sign S04 'Forbidden Turn' under Section 3d of this act, shall be guilty of forbidden turn

- (i) Forbidden turn is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Forbidden turn shall warrant a citation of $235

---

**FAILURE TO COMPLY WITH TRAFFIC SIGNAGE**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, and fails to follow the instructions of a sign, described by section 3 as a miscellaneous sign, or who fails to follow the instructions of a Traffic Guide, shall be guilty of failure to comply with traffic signature

- (i) Traffic signature is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Traffic signature shall warrant a citation of $245

---

**ILLEGAL PARKING**

Whoever parks their motor vehicle in such a way:

- (i) In a fire lane indicated by a red painted curb

- (ii) In front of a fire hydrant

- (iii) Within a bus stop, as described by sign S06 under section 3 of this act, except when operated by an on-duty employee of the Harrison transit authority

- (iv) On the interstate highway, including but not limited to the shoulder

- (v) Within ten (10) studs of an intersection, except when in a marked parking spot

- (vi) On an intersection

- (vii) On a sidewalk or pedestrian pathway

- (viii) Within a marked crosswalk

- (ix) In front of a driveway or private access point without permission from the owner or occupant

- (x) On a railroad crossing or railroad right-of-way

- (xi) On private property where parking is prohibited or without the consent of the Property owner

- (xii) On an unimproved surface within municipal limits where parking is not designated or permitted

- (xiii) On public piers or docks, except where parking is expressly authorized

- (xiv) In a no-parking or no-standing zone indicated by sign or roadway marking

- (xv) On a displacement pane

- (xvi) In a marked parking space while not aligned with the markings or parking direction

- (xvii) Occupying more than one marked parking space

- (xviii) With any portion of the vehicle extending into a travel lane, sidewalk, or restricted Area

- (xix) Blocking a designated emergency vehicle access route

- (xx) On vehicle spawn pads, unless moved within reasonable timeframe

Except if:

- (i) The motor vehicle is an emergency vehicle engaged in official duties

- (ii) The motor vehicle is parked entirely within the boundaries of a marked parking space, unless parking in such space is otherwise prohibited by this section

- (iii) The marked parking space is designed for partial parking on a sidewalk

Shall be guilty of illegal parking

- (i) Illegal parking is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Illegal parking shall warrant a citation of $125

---

**FAILURE TO STOP AT A RED LIGHT**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle and proceeds through, enters, or crosses an Intersection while a traffic control signal displays a steady red signal, as defined Under section 3 D01 "Traffic Light", shall be guilty of failure to stop at a red light

A complete stop shall be made before entering the intersection, at a marked stop line, Crosswalk, or if none present, at the point nearest to the intersection where the operator has a clear view of approaching traffic

Except if:

- (i) The operator comes to a complete stop and yields the right-of-way to all pedestrians and vehicular traffic lawfully using the intersection before making a permitted right turn

- (ii) The traffic control signal displaying a green or yellow flashing arrow governing the direction of travel taken by the motor vehicle

- (iii) A sign or roadway marking permits a right turn on red

Shall be guilty of failure to stop at a red light

- (i) Failure to stop at a red light is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Failure to stop at a red light shall warrant a citation of $325

---

**OBSTRUCTION OF AN EMERGENCY VEHICLE**

Whoever intentionally obstructs an emergency vehicle actively utilizing their Emergency lights, shall be guilty of obstruction of an emergency vehicle

- (i) Obstruction of an emergency vehicle is a misdemeanor-type offense

- (ii) Obstruction of an emergency vehicle warrants a citation of $565, or a county jail sentence of seven (6) minutes

---

**IMPEDING TRAFFIC**

Whoever, with intent, unlawfully obstructs the normal flow of traffic, including Drivers driving under the speed limit to a point where it disrupts normal traffic movement or operating a vehicle incapable of maintaining a speed within twenty (20) miles per hour of the speed limit of the road, shall be guilty of impeding on traffic

- (i) Impeding traffic shall be an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Impeding on traffic shall warrant a citation of $325

---

**FAILURE TO MAINTAIN LANE**

Any driver of a motor vehicle, who is unable to maintain a single lane of travel, swerves between lanes, invades the lanes of oncoming traffic for short periods of time or excessively swerves within their lane of travel shall be guilty of Failure to Maintain Lane. Any motor vehicle which changes lanes without proper use of indicators when changing lanes,

Except if:

- (i) Correctly indicating a change of lane

- (ii) The motor vehicle is an emergency vehicle

- (iii) The motor Vehicle is too wide to stay in a single lane or has an authorisation from the Harrison County Transit Authority to occupy more than one lane of travel

- (iv) The motor vehicle is carrying out Waste Disposal duties and is owned and operated by Harrison County Waste Management

- (v) The motor vehicle is carrying out delivery/postal-related duties and is owned and operated by the Harrison County Post Office.

Shall be guilty of Failure to Maintain Lane.

- (i) Failure to Maintain Lane is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Failure to Maintain Lane shall warrant a citation of $225

---

**DRIVING WITHOUT HEADLIGHTS**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle at night, without having their headlights Enabled, shall be guilty of driving without headlights

- (i) Driving without headlights is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Driving without headlights shall warrant a citation of $125

---

**UNLAWFUL OPERATION OF A MOTOR VEHICLE OFF DESIGNATED ROADWAYS**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle in any of the following ways:

- (i) On grass, dirt, sand, gravel, or any unimproved surface within municipal limits, not designed for vehicular travel

- (ii) Within public parks, green spaces, playgrounds, or recreational areas, except on Roadways or parking areas expressly intended for motor vehicles

- (iii) On pedestrian pathways, trails, or sidewalks not intended for motor vehicle use

- (iv) Across curbs, medians, embankments, or landscaped areas for the purpose of Bypassing roadways, traffic controls, or parking restrictions

- (v) In any area posted or designated as restricted to pedestrian, recreational, or non-vehicular use

Except if:

- (i) The motor vehicle is an emergency vehicle

- (ii) The motor vehicle is operated by the Transit Authority

- (iii) The motor vehicle is operated on private property with consent from the owner or Occupant

- (iv) The motor vehicle is operated in an area expressly designated for off-road vehicular use

Shall be guilty of unlawful operation of a motor vehicle off designed roadways

- (i) Unlawful operation of a motor vehicle off designed roadways is an infraction-type Offense

- (ii) Unlawful operation of a motor vehicle off designed roadways shall warrant a citation of $195

---

**NOISE POLLUTION**

Whoever operates a motor vehicle, whilst excessively utilizing said motor vehicle to Create noise within municipal limits, through means of their vehicle horn shall be Guilty of noise pollution

- (i) Noise pollution is an infraction-type offense

- (ii) Noise pollution shall warrant a citation of $225

---

## SECTION 9. SEVERABILITY

If any provision of this Act, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or act, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Act which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Act are declared to be severable.
