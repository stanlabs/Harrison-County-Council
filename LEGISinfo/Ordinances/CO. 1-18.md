# CO. 1-18 Non Emergency Public Safety Vehicles Ordinance

*Introduced June 16, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

June 16, 2026 Mr. gTheronll introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To protect non-emergency public safety vehicles and their operators from legal repercussion when operating in the interest of public safety.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as "Non Emergency Public Safety Vehicles Ordinance of 2026" or "The N.E.P.S.V. Ordinance of 2026".

---

## SECTION 2. SCOPE

(a) This Ordinance applies to the Harrison County Weather Service and the Harrison County Transportation Authority.

(b) The abbreviations "HCWS" and "HCTA" shall refer to the Harrison County Weather Service and The Harrison County Transportation Authority respectively.

---

## SECTION 3. NON-EMERGENCY PUBLIC SAFETY VEHICLE

(a) NON-EMERGENCY PUBLIC SAFETY VEHICLE - A Non-Emergency Public Safety Vehicle (NEPSV) shall be defined as:

> (i) Any county-owned vehicle engaged in the operations, support of, or built for the purpose of maintaining roadways;
>
> (ii) Any county-owned vehicle engaged in the operations, support of, or built for the purpose of chasing or tracking severe or dangerous weather phenomena.

(b) TEMPORARY DESIGNATION OF NEPSV - The following vehicles are temporarily designated as a NEPSV when;

> (i) A county-owned bus or other public transportation vehicle is actively engaged in otherwise reckless driving in order to preserve the life of its occupants.

---

## SECTION 4. PROTECTIONS FROM VEHICLE CODE

(a) COUNTY WEATHER SERVICE - Any official vehicle operated by the Harrison County Weather Service when actively chasing, observing or tracking severe weather phenomena including but not limited to; Thunderstorms, Tornadoes, Hailstorms, Derechos, Waterspouts, Hurricanes, Blizzards, Floods, Tropical Storms, Tropical Depressions, Wildfires, Avalanches, Storm Surge, and any other severe weather phenomena listed by the Harrison County Weather Service; SHALL not be subject to the following violations of the Vehicle Code;

> (i) 2 § 1002 Failure to Yield to an Emergency Vehicle; During a severe weather event; although Emergency Vehicles almost always are given priority over all other vehicles, first responders are not trained or versed in the meteorological knowledge required to exercise caution when tracking a severe weather phenomenon and thus must give right of way to HCWS vehicles during a severe weather event.
>
> (ii) 2 § 1004 Reckless Driving, 2 § 1005 Failure to Maintain Lane, 2 § 1006 Driving the Wrong Way, 2 § 1007 Speeding, 2 § 1008 Criminal Speeding, 2 § 1011 Failure to Indicate, 2 § 1001 Failure to Yield to a Traffic Control Device; During a severe weather event, it may become necessary for HCWS vehicles to break multiple traffic laws at one time in order to preserve the life of its occupants.

---

## SECTION 5. HARRISON COUNTY TRANSPORTATION AUTHORITY

(a) COUNTY TRANSPORTATION - Any official vehicle operated by the Harrison County Transportation SHALL not be subject to the following violations of the Vehicle Code; 2 § 1004 Reckless Driving, 2 § 1005 Failure to Maintain Lane, 2 § 1006 Driving the Wrong Way, 2 § 1007 Speeding, 2 § 1008 Criminal Speeding, 2 § 1011 Failure to Indicate, 2 § 1001 Failure to Yield to a Traffic Control Device; During any life-threatening situation when the life of the occupants of any bus, public transportation vehicle, or any county-owned vehicle engaged in the operations, support of, or built for the purpose of maintaining roadways, such as a shooting or when evading a severe weather phenomena.

---

## SECTION 6. ENFORCEMENT

(b) GOOD FAITH SAFE HARBOR - No employee shall be subject to criminal liability for actions taken in good faith reliance on a lawfully adopted ordinance.

(c) OFFICIAL MISCONDUCT - Any Law Enforcement Officer who knowingly stops or cites a NEPSV operator protected by the stipulations of this Ordinance shall be guilty of Official Misconduct.

(d) FALSE IMPRISONMENT - Any Law Enforcement Officer who knowingly arrests an operator of a NEPSV protected by the stipulations of this Ordinance shall be guilty of False Imprisonment

---

## SECTION 7. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
