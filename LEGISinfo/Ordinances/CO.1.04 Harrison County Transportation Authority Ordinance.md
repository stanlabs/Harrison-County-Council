# CO. 1-4 Harrison County Transportation Authority Ordinance

*Introduced February 4, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

February 4, 2026 Mr. Vinnyvxx introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To establish the Harrison County Transportation Authority; to provide for its organization, administration, and responsibilities; to authorize the appointment of a Director and personnel; and to provide for the planning, operation, and maintenance of County transportation infrastructure and transit services.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as the "Harrison County Transportation Authority Ordinance" or the "HCTA Ordinance".

---

## SECTION 2. HARRISON COUNTY TRANSPORTATION AUTHORITY; DIRECTOR

(a) There is hereby established an executive authority of Harrison County to be known as the Harrison County Transportation Authority ("HCTA" or the "Authority").

(b) The Authority shall be responsible for the planning, administration, construction, operation, maintenance, and stewardship of County transportation systems, infrastructure, services, and facilities connected therewith, including roadways, bridges, tunnels, rights-of-way, traffic control infrastructure, transit systems, rail infrastructure, depots, yards, terminals, maintenance facilities, parking facilities, and any other transportation-related property or improvements placed under its jurisdiction by County law or Resolution of the Council (collectively, "transportation infrastructure administered by HCTA").

(c) There shall be a Director of the Harrison County Transportation Authority, appointed by the County Executive and confirmed by the Council, who shall serve as the chief administrative officer of the Authority and exercise such authority as is necessary to carry out this Ordinance and other applicable County law.

(d) The Authority may establish, modify, or dissolve such divisions, offices, programs, and services as are necessary to carry out its responsibilities under this Ordinance.

---

## SECTION 3. POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

(a) The Authority may plan, construct, operate, maintain, repair, and improve the transportation infrastructure administered by HCTA, including responsibility for the maintenance and stewardship of rail infrastructure only; provided, however, that the Authority shall not operate trains or rail services, which shall be provided by the duly authorized rail operator, including General Eastern Railway, or its successor.

(b) The Authority may plan, provide, operate, or contract for public transit services, including bus, shuttle, circulator, and paratransit services, but shall not operate rail passenger service.

(c) The Authority may conduct non-enforcement traffic operations and roadway support activities, including but not limited to: traffic control, traffic management related to construction or special events, debris removal, assistance with disabled or abandoned vehicles, and coordination with public safety agencies.

(d) The Authority may operate, administer, or contract for towing and impound services related to the transportation infrastructure administered by HCTA.

(e) The Authority may establish and administer permit systems and administrative regulations governing activities affecting County transportation infrastructure, and may enforce permit conditions through administrative remedies authorized by County law.

(f) The Authority may enter into contracts, agreements, or memoranda of understanding with public or private entities as necessary to carry out its duties.

---

## SECTION 4. PERSONNEL; LIMITATION OF AUTHORITY

(a) The Director may appoint, employ, and remove such employees, contractors, and agents as necessary to carry out the functions of the Authority, in accordance with applicable law, the County Charter, and personnel policies adopted by the County.

(b) Nothing in this Ordinance shall be construed to grant the Authority or its personnel any police powers, law enforcement authority, arrest authority, citation authority, or public safety enforcement function. Employees of the Authority are not peace officers by virtue of this Ordinance.

---

## SECTION 5. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
