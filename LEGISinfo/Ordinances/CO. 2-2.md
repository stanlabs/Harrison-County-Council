# CO. 2-2 Fix the Game Ordinance

*Introduced June 29, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

June 29, 2026 — Mr. A0IM introduced the following Ordinance to the Council.

## AN ORDINANCE

To introduce the full criminal code to Harrison (finally).

*Be it resolved by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as "Fix the Game Ordinance"

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) PUBLIC SERVANT

> (i) Any person who is an on-duty employee of a government agency

---

## SECTION 3. CHAPTER 1: CRIMES AGAINST THE COUNTY

(a) TREASON

> (i) Whoever, with knowledge and forethought, wages war or provides material support to the enemies of Harrison County, shall be found guilty of Treason.
>
> (ii) Treason shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Treason shall warrant a jail sentence of 40 minutes
>
> (iv) Treason shall be a conviction-only offense

(b) PERJURY

> (i) Whoever, with knowledge and malice under oath, makes a false statement that materially impacts an official proceeding, shall be found guilty of Perjury.
>
> (ii) Perjury shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Perjury shall warrant a jail sentence of 12 minutes or a citation of $400
>
> (iv) Perjury shall be a conviction-only offense

(c) CONTEMPT OF COURT

> (i) A court of The County shall have power to punish by fine or imprisonment, or both, at its discretion, such contempt of its authority, and none other, as-Misbehavior of any person in its presence or so near thereto as to obstruct the administration of justice; Misbehavior of any of its officers in their official transactions; Disobedience or resistance to its lawful writ, process, order, rule, decree, or command.
>
> (ii) Contempt of court shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) The jail sentence or citation amount shall be set by the court of the county in which the violation of this offense has taken place

(d) OBSTRUCTION OF A PUBLIC SERVANT

> (i) Whoever, with knowledge of their actions, hindered, delayed, or interfered with the official lawful duties of a public servant without due authorization by law or right, shall be found guilty of Obstruction of a Public Servant.
>
> (ii) Obstruction of a public servant shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Obstruction of a public servant shall warrant a jail sentence of 8 minutes or a citation of $300

(e) IMPERSONATION OF A PUBLIC SERVANT

> (i) Whoever makes themselves appear in a manner similar to that of a public servant, with the intent of inducing another to submit to such pretended official authority, shall be found guilty of Impersonation of a Public Servant.
>
> (ii) Impersonation of a public servant shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Impersonation of a public servant shall warrant a jail sentence of 12 minutes

(f) FAILURE TO PAY CITATION

> (i) Whoever fails to pay a citation within 30 minutes of its issuance shall be found guilty of Failure to Pay Citation.
>
> (ii) Failure to pay citation shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Failure to pay citation shall warrant a jail sentence of 8 minutes
>
> (iv) Any person who is guilty of failure to pay citation, shall automatically be served with an arrest warrant for failure to pay citation

(g) ACCESSORY AFTER THE FACT TO A MISDEMEANOR

> (i) Whoever is aware that an individual committed a Misdemeanor, and deliberately helps the individual avoid arrest, prosecution, or punishment while not participating in the original Misdemeanor, shall be found guilty of Accessory After The Fact to a Misdemeanor.
>
> (ii) Accessory after the fact to a misdemeanor shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Accessory after the fact to a misdemeanor shall warrant a jail sentence of 10 minutes

(h) ACCESSORY AFTER THE FACT TO A FELONY

> (i) Whoever is aware that an individual committed a Felony, and deliberately helps the individual avoid arrest, prosecution, or punishment while not participating in the original Felony, shall be found guilty of Accessory After The Fact to a Felony.
>
> (ii) Accessory after the fact to a felony shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Accessory after the fact to a felony shall warrant a jail sentence of 15 minutes

(i) EVIDENCE TAMPERING

> (i) Whoever, with knowledge of an official proceeding or investigation, intentionally destroys, alters, hides, removes, steals, or creates physical evidence to impair the official proceeding or investigation, shall be found guilty of Evidence Tampering.
>
> (ii) Evidence tampering shall be a felony-class offense
>
> (iii) Evidence tampering shall warrant a jail sentence of 15 minutes

(j) UNAUTHORIZED PRACTICE OF LAW

> (i) Whoever practices law without a license granted by the State Bar shall be found guilty of Unauthorized Practice of Law.
>
> (ii) Unauthorized practice of law shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Unauthorized practice of law shall warrant a jail sentence of 8 minutes or a citation of $800
>
> (iv) Whoever is guilty of unauthorized practice of law, may additionally be found guilty of contempt of court

(k) ESCAPE

> (i) Whoever, with intent to leave a correctional facility without lawful authorization, shall be found guilty of Escape.
>
> (ii) Escape shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Escape shall warrant a jail sentence of 15 minutes

---

## SECTION 4. CHAPTER 2: CRIMES AGAINST THE PERSON

(a) ROBBERY

> (i) Whoever, with intent to deprive an individual of their property permanently, forcibly or with threat of violence, steals property in the presence or of the individual, shall be guilty of Robbery.
>
> (ii) Robbery shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Robbery shall warrant a jail sentence of 12 minutes

(b) AGGRAVATED ROBBERY

> (i) Whoever commits Robbery while utilizing a deadly weapon or threatening the use thereof shall be guilty of Aggravated Robbery.
>
> (ii) Aggravated robbery shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Aggravated robbery shall warrant a jail sentence of 16 minutes

(c) FALSE IMPRISONMENT

> (i) Whoever, with intent, unlawfully restrains, detains, or confines an individual without consent, shall be guilty of False Imprisonment.
>
> (ii) False imprisonment shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) False imprisonment shall warrant a jail sentence of 10 minutes

(d) BATTERY

> (i) Whoever, with intent to cause harm through unconsensual contact, shall be guilty of Battery.
>
> (ii) Battery shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Battery shall warrant a jail sentence of 10 minutes

(e) CRIMINAL THREATS

> (i) Whoever, with malice, causes a threat of violence in word, writing, or electronic communication, with intent that it be received as a threat or that a reasonable individual would believe it to be real or pressing, shall be guilty of Criminal Threats.
>
> (ii) Criminal threats shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Criminal threats shall warrant a jail sentence of 8 minutes

---

## SECTION 5. CHAPTER 3: CRIMES AGAINST PROPERTY

(a) VEHICLE THEFT

> (i) Whoever, with the intent to permanently deprive the individual of their vehicle, knows that the vehicle doesn't belong to them, and takes the vehicle without the owner's consent, shall be found guilty of Vehicle Theft.
>
> (ii) Vehicle theft shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Vehicle theft shall warrant a jail sentence of 12 minutes

(b) THEFT

> (i) Whoever, with the intent to permanently deprive the owner of their property, knows that such property doesn't belong to them, and takes the property without the owner's consent, shall be found guilty of Theft.
>
> (ii) Theft shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Theft shall warrant a jail sentence of 8 minutes or a citation of $300

(c) FELONY THEFT

> (i) Whoever, while committing Theft, deprives the owner of property exceeding $500 in value; or deprives the owner of a firearm, shall be found guilty of Felony Theft.
>
> (ii) Felony theft shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Felony theft shall warrant a jail sentence of 12 minutes or a citation of $900

(d) BURGLARY

> (i) Whoever enters a property without consent of the owner, with the intent to commit a crime inside the property, shall be found guilty of Burglary.
>
> (ii) Burglary shall be a felony-type offense
>
> (iii) Burglary shall warrant a jail sentence of 12 minutes

(e) BREAKING AND ENTERING

> (i) Whoever forcefully enters the real property of another without consent of the owner shall be found guilty of Breaking and Entering.
>
> (ii) Breaking and entering shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Breaking and entering shall warrant a jail sentence of 10 minutes

(f) VANDALISM

> (i) Whoever deliberately defaces or destroys the property of the owner without consent shall be found guilty of Vandalism.
>
> (ii) Vandalism shall be a misdemeanor-type offense
>
> (iii) Vandalism shall warrant a jail sentence of 4 minutes or a citation of $250

---

## SECTION 6. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
