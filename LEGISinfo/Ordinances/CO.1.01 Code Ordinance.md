# CO. 1-1 Harrison Code of Statutes Ordinance

*Introduced February 2nd, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

February 2nd, 2026 — Mr. King_v123 introduced the following Bill to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To define the Code of Statutes and its Titles.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as the "Code Ordinance".

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) The term "Reform program" shall refer to a program conducted by a Government Agency that is designed to let a person expunge their criminal record by proving good behavior.

---

## SECTION 3. HARRISON CODE OF STATUTES

(a) The Harrison Code of Statutes shall be a document containing all relevant penal codes and shall be further expanded into titles as further defined by this Ordinance.

(b) Pursuant to Section 29 of the County Charter this Ordinance shall establish the Criminal Code, a document containing all criminal offenses established by law. It shall be considered Title 1 of the Harrison Code of Statutes.

- (i) The Criminal Code shall be further expanded into the following Chapters:

  - (1) Chapter I — Crimes Against The County
  - (2) Chapter II — Crimes Against The Person
  - (3) Chapter III — Crimes Against Public Safety
  - (4) Chapter IV — Inchoate Crimes

- (ii) The Criminal Code shall be cited as H.C.C. § XYYY with X standing for Chapter (1–4) and YYY standing for a particular offense within the Chapter (001–999).

  - (1) H.C.C. may be substituted with 1, standing for Title 1

(c) A Vehicle Code shall be established as Title 2 of the Harrison Code of Statutes. It shall contain all offenses pertaining to use of vehicles and regulation of traffic.

- (i) The Vehicle Code shall be further expanded into the following Chapters:

  - (1) Chapter I — Vehicle Code

- (ii) The Vehicle Code shall be cited as H.V.C. § XYYY with X standing for Chapter (1) and YYY standing for a particular offense within the Chapter (001–999).

  - (1) H.V.C. may be substituted with 2, standing for Title 2

(d) A Park Code shall be established as Title 3 of the Harrison Code of Statutes. It shall contain all offenses pertaining to criminal behavior within the County Parks.

- (i) The Park Code shall be further expanded into the following Chapters:

  - (1) Chapter I — Recreational Usage of Park Facilities
  - (2) Chapter II — Prohibited Vehicle Conduct

- (ii) The Park Code shall be cited as H.P.C. § XYYY with X standing for Chapter (1–2) and YYY standing for a particular offense within the Chapter (001–999).

  - (1) H.P.C. may be substituted with 3, standing for Title 3

- (iii) The Park Director may submit amendments to the Park Code. These amends shall be voted by the County Council as if they have been submitted by a councilor.

(e) A Fire Code shall be established as Title 4 of the Harrison Code of Statutes. It shall contain all offenses pertaining to criminal behaviour concerned with fire safety.

- (i) The Fire Code shall be further expanded into the following Chapters:

  - (1) Chapter I — Crimes Involving Fire
  - (2) Chapter II — Crimes Involving Public Safety

- (ii) The Fire Code shall be cited as H.F.C. § XYYY with X standing for Chapter (1–2) and YYY standing for a particular offense within the Chapter (001–999).

  - (1) H.F.C. may be substituted with 4, standing for Title 4

- (iii) The Fire Chief may submit amendments to the Fire Code. These amends shall be voted by the County Council as if they have been submitted by a councilor.

(f) An offense may fall under the three following categories:

- (i) **Infraction** — reserved for minor offenses. Infractions may only be punished with a monetary fine not exceeding $1,000.

- (ii) **Misdemeanor** — reserved for more serious offenses. Misdemeanors may be punished with a monetary fine not exceeding $2,000 and up to 10 minutes jail time.

- (iii) **Felony** — reserved for the most serious offenses. Felonies may be punished with a monetary fine not exceeding $5,000 and up to 30 minutes jail time.

- (iv) Any offense exceeding 10 minutes jail time shall automatically be considered a felony.

(g) No government agency may hire a person that has been charged with, and therefore having an in-game record, or convicted of a Felony in the previous six months.

(h) No government agency may hire a person that has been charged with, and therefore having an in-game record, or convicted of more than three Misdemeanors in the previous three months.

(i) Provisions from Section 3 (g) and (h) shall not apply to Government endorsed reform programs.

---

## SECTION 4. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
