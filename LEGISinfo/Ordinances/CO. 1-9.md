# CO. 1-9 The Misconduct Ordinance

*Introduced January 20th, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

January 20th, 2026 Mr. King_v123 introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To define the criminal offence of Misconduct.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as "The Misconduct Ordinance ".

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) For the purposes of this Ordinance the term "Government employee" shall refer to any person, hired and employed by a Government Agency who currently holds a position within said Agency OR an individual occupying a Government Position.

(b) The term "Misconduct" shall be defined as intentional dereliction from duty, injurious to another on the part of one employed in a professional capacity, as a government employee, which does NOT result in a threat to life.

(c) The term "Gross Misconduct" shall be defined as intentional dereliction from duty, injurious to another, on the part of one employed in a professional capacity, as a government employee, which does result in a threat to life.

(d) The term "Dereliction from duty" shall be defined as a person's intentional or accidental failure to perform an obligation without a valid excuse, especially an obligation attached to their job.

(i) For an act to be considered a dereliction, its perpetrator has to willingly or negligently fail to perform his or her duties or perform such duties in a culpably inefficient manner.

(1) The work done in a culpably inefficient manner is work done in a manner where there is no reasonable or just excuse for the inefficiency.

(ii) Abuse of one's position regardless of whether the perpetrator benefits from said abuse shall fall under dereliction.

(iii) The injury-to-another element may be an injury towards an entity, agency, the State, as well as toward an individual or property, be it a physical injury, monetary injury, reputational injury, injury against dignity or injury against a legal right.

---

## SECTION 3. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT

(a) "Gross Misconduct" shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 40 minutes in the County Jail.

(b) "Misconduct" shall be considered a Felony, and shall result in 20 minutes in the County Jail or a $5,000 fine.

(c) The District Attorney's Office shall have primary jurisdiction in all cases involving violations of this Ordinance.

(d) Should a trier of fact in a case involving misconduct return a "Not Guilty" verdict for a higher degree of misconduct, they may, upon their own discretion, return a "Guilty" verdict for any lower degrees of misconduct should said verdict comply with double jeopardy rules, regardless of if the lower level was initially charged.

(i) The Degrees of misconduct, from highest to lowest, shall be as follows: Gross Misconduct, Misconduct.

(e) A person convicted of any degree of misconduct may not exercise the powers granted to them by their position for a period of 60 days from the date of the conviction.

(i) Upon the trier of fact's discretion this period may be extended or shortened by up to 30 days.

---

## SECTION 4. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
