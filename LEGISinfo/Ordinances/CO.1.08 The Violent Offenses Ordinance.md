# CO. 1-8 The Violent Offenses Ordinance

*Introduced January 26th, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

January 26th, 2026 Mr. King_v123 introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To define the violent criminal offenses.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as "The Violent Offenses Ordinance".

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) The term "Criminal Homicide" shall be defined as purposely, knowingly, recklessly or negligently causing the death of another human being. Criminal homicide is further divided into murder, manslaughter or negligent homicide.

(b) The term "Deadly Weapon" shall be defined as any firearm or other weapon, device, instrument, material or substance, whether animate or inanimate, which in the manner it is used or is intended to be used is known to be capable of producing death or serious bodily injury.

---

## SECTION 3. CRIMINAL HOMICIDE

(a) Criminal Homicide constitutes Murder when:

(i) It is committed purposely or knowingly; OR

(ii) It is committed under circumstances manifesting extreme indifference to the value of human life. Such recklessness and indifference are presumed if the actor is engaged or is an accomplice in the commission of a felony.

(b) Murder shall be further expanded into two degrees:

(i) "First Degree Murder" shall be defined as intentional and premeditated Murder, or a Murder of an On-Duty Peace Officer, Judge or a Justice or a Murder committed during the commission of a felony.

(1) It shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 30 minutes in the County Jail.

(ii) "Second Degree Murder" shall be defined as intentional and non-premeditated Murder.

(1) It shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 25 minutes in the County Jail.

(c) Criminal Homicide constitutes Manslaughter when:

(i) It is committed recklessly; OR

(ii) a homicide which would otherwise be murder is committed under the influence of extreme mental or emotional disturbance for which there is reasonable explanation or excuse.

The reasonableness of such explanation or excuse shall be determined.

from the viewpoint of a person in the actor's situation

under the circumstances as he believes them to be.

(d) "Manslaughter" shall be defined as reckless criminal homicide.

(i) It shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 20 minutes in the County Jail.

(e) Criminal homicide constitutes Negligent Homicide when:

(i) It is committed negligently.

(f) "Negligent Homicide" shall be defined as negligent criminal homicide.

(i) It shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 15 minutes in the County Jail.

(g) Should a trier of fact in a case involving Criminal Homicide return a "Not Guilty" verdict

for a higher degree of Criminal Homicide, they may, upon their own discretion, return a "Guilty" verdict for any lower degrees of Criminal Homicide should said verdict comply with double jeopardy rules, regardless of if the lower level was initially charged.

(i) The Degrees of Criminal Homicide, from highest to lowest, shall be as follows: First-Degree Murder, Second-Degree Murder, Manslaughter, Negligent Homicide

(h) "Justifiable Homicide" shall be defined as homicide done during the defense of self or

another person from real and imminent bodily injury or as otherwise justifiable by law.

(i) It shall not be considered as a criminal act.

---

## SECTION 4. OTHER VIOLENT OFFENSES

(a) "Assault" shall be divided into two degrees: "Simple Assault" and "Aggravated Assault"

(b) "Simple Assault" shall be defined as attempting to cause or purposely, knowingly or recklessly causing bodily injury to another.

(i) It shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 5 minutes in the County Jail or a $500 fine.

(c) "Aggravated Assault" shall be defined as attempting to cause serious bodily injury to another or purposely, knowingly or recklessly causing serious bodily injury to another.

(i) It shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 10 minutes in the County Jail.

(d) A person shall be guilty of Aggravated Assault when they:

(i) attempt to cause serious bodily injury to another, or cause such injury purposely, knowingly or recklessly under circumstances manifesting extreme indifference to the value of human life; OR

(ii) attempts to cause or purposely or knowingly causes bodily injury to another with a deadly weapon.

(e) "Kidnapping" shall be defined as intentional and unlawful transportation of a person over significant distances without their consent.

(i) It shall be considered a Felony and shall result in 10 minutes in the County Jail.

(f) A person shall be guilty of Kidnapping when they:

(i) unlawfully remove another from his place of residence or business or a substantial distance from the vicinity where he is found; OR

(ii) if he unlawfully confines another for a substantial period in a place of isolation.

(g) Should a trier of fact in a case involving Assault return a "Not Guilty" verdict for a higher degree of Assault, they may, upon their own discretion, return a "Guilty" verdict for any lower degrees of Assault should said verdict comply with double jeopardy rules, regardless of if the lower level was initially charged.

(i) The Degrees of Assault from highest to lowest, shall be as follows: Aggravated Assault, Simple Assault.

## SECTION 5. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
