# CO. 1-10 The Inchoate Offenses Ordinance

*Introduced January 20th, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

January 20th, 2026 Mr. King_v123 introduced the following Ordinance to the Council

## AN ORDINANCE

To define the Inchoate Offenses and Aiding in a crime.

*Be it enacted by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as "The Inchoate Offenses Ordinance".

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) The term "Attempt to Commit a Crime" shall be defined as attempting to commit a criminal offense, acting with the culpability otherwise required for commission of the crime, taking a substantial step toward the crime, but ultimately abandoning or failing to do so.

(b) The term "Solicitation to Commit a Crime" shall be defined as commanding, encouraging or requesting another person to engage in criminal activity.

(c) The term "Conspiracy to Commit a Crime" shall be defined as planning or participating in a plan to commit a crime with another person or persons.

---

## SECTION 3. DOCUMENTATION; ENFORCEMENT

(a) A person shall be guilty of "Attempt to Commit a Crime" if they acting with the kind of culpability otherwise required for commission of the crime:

(i) Take a substantial step toward commission of the crime BUT are:

(1) Stopped before they are able to carry out the entirety of the crime, OR

(2) Not met with the intended result of their intended criminal plan.

(ii) Take a substantial step toward commission of the crime that would constitute the crime if attendant circumstances were as they believed them to be.

(iii) It shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 5 minutes in the County Jail or a $500 fine.

(b) A person shall be guilty of "Solicitation to Commit a Crime" if, with the purpose of promoting or facilitating a crime, they command, encourage, or request another person to engage in conduct that would constitute a crime or an attempt to commit a crime.

(i) It shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 5 minutes in the County Jail or a $500 fine.

(c) A person shall be guilty of "Conspiracy to Commit a Crime" if, with the purpose of promoting or facilitating the commission of a crime, they:

(i) agrees with another person or persons that they or one or more of them will engage in conduct that constitutes such crime or an attempt or solicitation to commit such crime, OR

(ii) agree to aid such other person or persons in the planning or commission of such crime or of an attempt or solicitation to commit such crime.

(iii) It shall be considered a Misdemeanor and shall result in 5 minutes in the County Jail or a $500 fine.

(1) All parties engaged in a particular conspiracy to commit a crime may be tried jointly in a court of law.

(d) A person aiding, assisting, or facilitating another person in commission of a crime shall be punished for the same crime as the person they have been aiding, assisting or facilitating.

---

## SECTION 4. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
