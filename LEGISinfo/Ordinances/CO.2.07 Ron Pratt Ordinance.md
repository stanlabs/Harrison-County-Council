# CO. 2-7 Ron Pratt Ordinance

*Introduced July 6, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

July 6, 2026 — Mr. glarpenn introduced the following Ordinance to the Council.

## AN ORDINANCE

To define the powers, protections, and limitations of motor vehicle towing and impounding.

*Be it resolved by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as the "Ron Pratt Ordinance"

---

## SECTION 2. DEFINITIONS

(a) ILLEGALLY PARKED VEHICLE

- (i) Any vehicle parked in a manner that is defined as 'Illegal Parking' in Title II, Section 1016(a) of the Harrison County Code of Statutes.

(b) LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER

- (i) Any sworn law enforcement officer employed by the Jamestown Police Department or Harrison County Sheriff's Office or serving in the Harrison County Militia and assigned to a military police detachment.

---

## SECTION 3. TOWING BY OPERATOR DISCRETION

(a) Employees of the Harrison County Transportation Authority may tow a vehicle at their own discretion if the vehicle is an illegally parked vehicle, unless:

- (i) the vehicle is an emergency vehicle engaged in official duties; or

- (ii) the vehicle is occupied, in which case the occupant shall be directed to, if the driver, relocate the vehicle, or, if a passenger, exit the vehicle.

---

## SECTION 4. TOWING BY SPECIAL REQUEST

(a) Employees of the Harrison County Transportation Authority may tow a vehicle upon receiving special request from:

- (i) Law enforcement officers, when

  - (1) the vehicle was used in the commission of a crime, or

  - (2) the vehicle may be towed under the provisions of Section 3.

- (ii) Personnel of the Harrison County Militia, when

  - (1) the vehicle is parked in a manner that allows for access to a military installation,

  - (2) the vehicle is parked within the boundaries of a military installation,

  - (3) the vehicle is obstructing access to and from a military installation, or

  - (4) the vehicle may be towed under the provisions of Section 3.

- (iii) Employees of Harrison County Fire and Rescue, when

  - (1) the vehicle is on fire and requires relocation to be extinguished, or

  - (2) the vehicle was involved in a motor vehicle accident and is disabled, or

  - (3) the vehicle may be towed under the provisions of Section 3.

- (iv) The registered owner of the vehicle, for any reason deemed appropriate and necessary by the Harrison County Transportation Authority.

---

## SECTION 5. IMPEDING AND OBSTRUCTING

(a) OBSTRUCTION OF A PUBLIC SERVANT

- (i) Any person who unlawfully obstructs or impedes towing or impounding operations as outlined in this ordinance shall be guilty of "Obstruction of a Public Servant."

- (ii) Obstruction of a Public Servant shall be a misdemeanor-type offense.

- (iii) Obstruction of a Public Servant shall warrant a fine of $500 or a jail sentence of 5 minutes.

---

## SECTION 6. LIABILITIES

(a) The Harrison County Transportation Authority or its employees shall not be held criminally or civilly liable for acting in accordance with the provisions of this ordinance.

(b) The Harrison County Transportation Authority or its employees, acting upon the request of any person under Section 4, except when they knowingly conduct their duties in violation of the law, shall not be held criminally or civilly liable.

- (i) The person requesting authorization for towing or impounding the vehicle shall be liable if the impound/tow was deemed unlawful. Employees of the Harrison County Transportation Authority, when acting in accordance with Section 3, may be held liable if the vehicle was not requested or authorized for towing under a person authorized in Section 4.

(c) If the employee of the Harrison County Transportation Authority cannot verify through game mechanics the owner of the vehicle, they may not be held liable, provided that it would be reasonable to believe the requesting party.

---

## SECTION 7. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable.
