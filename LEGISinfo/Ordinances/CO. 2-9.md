# CO. 2-9 Council Clerk's Office Ordinance

*Introduced July 6, 2026*

---

IN THE COUNCIL OF HARRISON COUNTY

July 6, 2026 Mr. Vinnyvxx introduced the following Ordinance to the Council.

## AN ORDINANCE

To establish the Office of the County Council Clerk.

*Be it resolved by the County Council in accordance with Section 24 of the County Charter,*

---

## SECTION 1. CITATION

(a) This Ordinance shall be cited as "County Clerk's Office Ordinance"

---

## SECTION 2. ESTABLISHMENT OF THE COUNCIL CLERK'S OFFICE; DUTIES

(a) There is hereby established within the Harrison County Council the Office of the County Council Clerk, which may be referred to as the "Council Clerk's Office."

(b) The Council Clerk's Office shall be headed by the County Council Clerk, who may be referred to as the "Council Clerk" or "Clerk," and who shall be appointed by the County Executive.

(c) The Council Clerk's Office shall serve as the principal administrative and legislative support office of the County Council and shall be responsible for docketing, recordkeeping, publication, certification, and the processing of official Council business.

(d) The Council Clerk's Office may:

> (i) Assist with the administration, organization, documentation, and procedural support of Council meetings, hearings, sessions, committees, and other official proceedings;
>
> (ii) Maintain custody of official Council records, filings, archives, legislative history, and other official materials, and provide access to such materials as required or authorized by law, rule, or Council policy;
>
> (iii) Issue and enforce administrative procedures, filing instructions, docketing requirements, formatting standards, recordkeeping practices, and internal office policies necessary for the orderly administration of Council business, provided such procedures are consistent with law;
>
> (iv) Make non-substantive corrections to submitted documents and legislation, including corrections to grammar, spelling, formatting, numbering, citations, typographical errors, section references, and similar clerical matters, provided such corrections do not materially alter the legal effect, substance, or intent of the document or legislation; and
>
> (v) Perform such other reasonable functions as the Clerk determines necessary for the orderly administration of Council business, and any other functions assigned by law, ordinance, resolution, Council rule, policy, or lawful directive of the County Council or County Executive.

(e) Service as Council Clerk or within the Council Clerk's Office shall be open to any qualified person not otherwise prohibited by law, provided that any person serving within the Office shall perform their duties in a neutral, administrative, and clerical capacity and shall not use the Office's authority, records, staff, resources, or functions for an improper personal, political, or legislative purpose.

---

## SECTION 3. STAFF OF THE COUNCIL CLERK'S OFFICE

(a) The County Council Clerk may appoint, hire, assign, supervise, train, discipline, suspend, demote, remove, or terminate staff of the Council Clerk's Office, who shall serve at the pleasure of the County Council Clerk, as necessary for the efficient administration of the Office.

(b) The County Council Clerk may establish, designate, modify, or abolish internal positions, titles, ranks, duties, functions, responsibilities, and chains of supervision within the Council Clerk's Office.

(c) The County Council Clerk may assign duties to staff, issue lawful directives, adopt internal procedures, delegate clerical or administrative functions, and take other reasonable personnel actions necessary to manage the Council Clerk's Office.

(d) The County Council may, by resolution, remove any subordinate staff member of the Council Clerk.

---

## SECTION 4. SEVERABILITY

(a) If any provision of this Ordinance, or the application thereof to any person, office, circumstance, or Ordinance, is held invalid, unconstitutional, or unenforceable by a Court of Justice or competent jurisdiction, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions or applications of this Ordinance which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application, and to this end the provisions of this Ordinance are declared to be severable
