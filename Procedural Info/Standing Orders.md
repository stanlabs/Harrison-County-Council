 # 2nd Harrison County Council Rules of Proceeding

*Adopted July 6, 2026*

*Last revised ---*

---

## (1) Preamble

Pursuant to Article III, Section 18 of the County Charter, the Harrison County Council shall adopt the following Rules of Proceeding.

---

## (2) Chairman

The Chairman is the presiding officer over a session of the Harrison County Council.

- The County Executive (CE) shall be the primary Chairman of the County Council.
- When the County Executive is absent, the President Pro Tempore (PPT) shall serve as Chairman.
- If neither the CE nor the PPT is present, a Chairman shall be appointed by simple majority vote at the beginning of the session.
- As the first order of business at the beginning of each term, the County Council shall appoint a President Pro Tempore by majority vote.
- The County Council may, through resolution, remove the current PPT provided there is just reason.

---

## (3) Journal of Record

The Journal of Record shall contain:

1. Date and time of sessions convening and adjourning.
2. Register of present members.
3. Motions.
4. Orders of the Chairman.
5. Votes.

The Journal of Record shall not contain transcripts of speeches that are irrelevant.

- The County Council shall appoint a Clerk at the beginning of every term.
- The County Council may remove a Clerk at any time with just reason.

---

## (4) Sessions and Quorum

- The quorum requirement for normal business shall be a majority of the membership as prescribed by the County Charter.
- The membership shall convene in regular sessions at least once every two weeks.
- Special sessions shall be convened following a majority of the councilors.
- Special sessions may be called by any single councilor but must be of an urgent nature.

At the convening of a special session:

- The councilor who originally called the session shall quickly discuss the subjects designated in the call.
- Only those designated subjects shall be discussed.
- All such subjects must be of an urgent nature.

---

## (5) Order of Business

### First Meeting of a New Term

1. Elect the first President Pro Tempore as prescribed by the County Charter.
2. Ratify the Rules of Proceeding.

### Regular Sessions

1. Roll shall be called and the register of participating members taken.
2. Unfinished business from a previous session shall be discussed.
3. Members shall:
   - Listen to other members;
   - Confirm appointments; and
   - Hear acts or resolutions that have been tabled.

   The Chairman or Clerk shall read the title unless the entire text is required to be read.

4. All acts and resolutions shall be voted upon in chronological order of submission to the docket. Councilors may bring tabled acts or resolutions to the floor.
5. Sessions shall close when adjournment is made from the floor of the County Council.

### Special Sessions

1. Roll shall be called and the register of participating members taken.
2. The member calling the special session shall briefly explain:
   - The issue at hand; and
   - Why the session is urgent.
3. Relevant acts and resolutions shall be voted upon.
4. The session shall be adjourned when:
   - Adjournment is made from the floor; or
   - All relevant acts and resolutions have been voted upon.

---

## (6) Rules of Debate

When a member desires to speak, they shall rise and address the Chairman:

> Mr./Madam Chairman, County Executive, or President Pro Tempore

The member shall not proceed until recognized by the Chairman.

- Once recognized, the member shall not be forcefully required to yield the floor unless the County Council calls the question.
- Only one member may hold the floor at any given time.
- Every member shall avoid personal attacks and sit down when finished.
- No member shall speak out of place without leave from the Chairman.
- When two or more members rise simultaneously, the Chairman shall determine who receives the floor.

### Discipline

Members repeatedly violating these Rules shall be called to order by the Chairman and may be ordered to take their seat.

A member called to order:

- Loses the right to speak on the pending motion;
- Is not barred from voting;
- Shall remain seated until:
  - Another motion is under consideration; or
  - The Chairman restores speaking privileges.

If a member refuses to take their seat immediately after being called to order, the Chairman shall name that member, who shall be escorted out by a deputy of the Sheriff's Office.

### Permitted Interruptions

A member may interrupt another only:

1. In compliance with a motion to order;
2. To doubt the presence of a quorum; or
3. To ask the speaker to yield.

### Limits on Debate

- No member shall speak more than once on a question unless all other members have been afforded that opportunity.
- No member shall occupy more than five minutes at a time where debate is unlimited.
- Members shall remain courteous, avoid personal attacks and digressions, and direct remarks to the Chairman.

---

## (7) Members of the Harrison County Council

- Any member who loses residency shall lose their seat on the County Council.
- Any member who commits a felony while in office may be subject to expulsion proceedings.

---

## (8) Motions in Order

- Motions shall only be entertained when the Chairman deems it suitable, except for points of order and parliamentary procedure.
- Only motions that permit interruption of the Chairman may allow members to speak without recognition.
- Motions may only be made after recognition by the Chairman.
- If a motion requires a second, the Chairman shall seek one.
- If the required second is not obtained, the motion fails automatically.

After a motion is seconded:

1. The Chairman shall seek opposition.
2. If no opposition is heard, the motion is adopted automatically.
3. If a member objects, they shall briefly state why.
4. The Chairman may refuse a motion only if it violates these Rules.
5. If opposition exists, the Chairman may:
   - Allow debate; or
   - Call the motion for a vote.

Results shall be announced as either:

- "The motion is adopted."
- "The motion is defeated."

- Questions for debate;
- Ordinances;
- Removal proceedings;
- Resolutions; and
- Similar business.

Other motions not listed may be accepted at the discretion of the Chairman and shall require a second and a simple majority to pass.

If no Clerk is present, the Chairman shall continue the session without one.

---

## (9) Introduction of Acts, Resolutions, or Nominations

Any member wishing to introduce an act, resolution, or nomination shall publish the draft at least 24 hours before the motion is made.

- Repealing signed legislation requires a simple majority of members.
- All acts shall receive consideration of amendments and amendments to amendments in a single reading.
- The Chairman or Clerk shall update the Trello whenever an act, resolution, or nomination is introduced.
- The County Executive, President Pro Tempore, or Clerk shall assign numbers and complete any highlighted template fields.

### Proxy Voting

If a councilor has a planned absence or cannot attend a planned session but wishes to vote on business expected to come before the Council, they may notify the Council of their vote through the `#Proxy-Request` channel.

---

## (10) Voting

- The Chairman shall declare the result of all voted motions.
- If any member doubts the result, the Chairman shall order the Clerk to conduct a roll call.

### Voting Method

- Votes shall be taken by voice vote unless:
  - Requested by any member; or
  - Directed by the Chairman.

In those cases, a roll-call vote shall be conducted.

### Abstentions

- A member requires permission from the Chairman to abstain.
- A member who is away from their keyboard shall be recorded as abstaining by the Clerk.
- Upon returning, that member shall be required to change their vote.

### Reporting Results

The Clerk or Chairman shall report vote outcomes using the format:

```text
Ayes – Nayes – Abstentions
```

### Reconsideration

When a question has been decided, any member voting with the prevailing side, or any member who did not vote, may move for reconsideration:

- On the same day; or
- During the next session.

If reconsideration is denied, withdrawn, or the original decision is affirmed, no further motion to reconsider shall be in order.

All motions to reconsider require a majority vote.

---

## (11) Chambers

The business of the membership shall be conducted in the County Council Room within Jamestown County Hall, located in the municipality of Jamestown.

- Meetings shall be open to the public unless the Chairman orders the meeting room sealed.
- The Chairman shall make reasonable arrangements for the press.
- Only individuals with pre-approved press passes may enter.

---

## (12) Amendments to the Rules of Proceeding

Any proposal to amend these Rules shall:

1. Be submitted in writing to the County Executive or President Pro Tempore.
2. Be provided with at least one day's notice.

No amendment shall take effect unless a majority of the entire County Council agrees.
